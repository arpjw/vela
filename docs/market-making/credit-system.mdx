The Vela credit system allows market makers to quote across multiple markets simultaneously using a single collateral deposit. Instead of requiring capital equal to the total value of all open orders, the credit system extends a configurable multiple of deposited collateral as quoting capacity.

## The Problem It Solves

A market maker quoting 11 markets needs to reserve capital for each order on each market. Without a credit system, quoting 5,000 USDC on each side of 11 markets requires 110,000 USDC in reserved capital. This is capital-inefficient — in practice, all orders never fill simultaneously.

The credit system solves this by treating deposited collateral as margin for a larger quoting envelope. A market maker depositing 10,000 USDC with a 5× credit ratio can quote up to 50,000 USDC of total order value across all markets.

## How It Works

### Credit Ratio

Each market maker account has a configured **credit ratio** (also called the leverage multiplier). This determines how much total quoted value you can maintain relative to your deposit:

```
max_quoted_value = deposited_collateral × credit_ratio
```

**Example:** 10,000 USDC deposited, credit ratio = 5×
- Max quoted value = 10,000 × 5 = 50,000 USDC
- You can maintain up to 50,000 USDC of open order value across all markets

### Credit Utilization

At any point in time, your **credit utilization** is:

```
total_quoted_value = Σ(price_i × size_i) for all open orders
credit_utilization = total_quoted_value / max_quoted_value
```

**Example:** You have open orders with a total value of 32,000 USDC. Your max quoted value is 50,000 USDC.
- Credit utilization = 32,000 / 50,000 = 64%

### Warning States

| Utilization | State | UI Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| 0–79% | Normal | Green |
| 80–94% | Amber warning | Yellow — reduce orders |
| 95–99% | Critical | Red — auto-cancel imminent |
| 100% | Auto-cancel threshold | Order cancelled atomically |

The MM dashboard shows a credit gauge (SVG arc) that updates in real time as orders are placed, filled, and cancelled.

## Auto-Cancel Mechanism

The auto-cancel is the core safety mechanism of the credit system. It is **atomic** — it fires at the exact moment a fill would push utilization to or past 1.0.

### How auto-cancel works:

1. An incoming order matches against one of your resting orders
2. Before committing the fill, the engine checks your projected utilization after the fill
3. If the fill would push utilization ≥ 1.0, the engine identifies your order with the lowest priority (worst price, oldest time) that can be cancelled to bring utilization back within bounds
4. The cancellation and fill happen in the same atomic operation
5. Your cancelled order's reserved funds are released; the fill proceeds

This ensures that a market maker's credit utilization invariant is never violated, even under rapid, concurrent matching.

<Warning>
If you receive an unexpected `CREDIT_BREACH` error when placing a new order, your current open orders are consuming enough of your credit quota that there is no room for the new order. Cancel some resting orders or increase your deposit.
</Warning>

## Mathematical Invariant

The credit system maintains the following invariant at all times:

```
total_quoted_value ≤ deposited_collateral × credit_ratio
```

This is enforced:
1. **On order placement:** New orders are rejected if they would push `total_quoted_value` past the cap
2. **On fill:** Auto-cancel fires atomically if a fill would cause a breach

### Proof of Atomic Consistency

Because the matching engine is single-threaded, there is no race condition between the fill and the auto-cancel. Both operations happen in the same engine tick, in sequence, before any state is committed. This is a key design advantage of the single-threaded architecture.

## Credit Parameters

| Parameter | Description | Set By |
|---|---|---|
| `credit_ratio` | Multiplier on deposited collateral | Operator (per account) |
| `max_quoted_value` | Absolute cap on total quoted value | Derived: `deposit × ratio` |
| `amber_threshold` | Utilization level for amber warning | Default: 0.80 |
| `critical_threshold` | Utilization level for critical warning | Default: 0.95 |

Credit parameters are configured by the operator at account creation. A future release will allow market makers to request ratio adjustments through the dashboard.

## Example: Multi-Market Quoting

A market maker deposits 20,000 USDC with a 10× credit ratio. Max quoted value = 200,000 USDC.

They quote two-sided on 5 markets:

| Market | Bid (Price × Size) | Ask (Price × Size) |
|---|---|---|
| BTC-USDC | 60,000 × 0.1 = 6,000 | 60,100 × 0.1 = 6,010 |
| ETH-USDC | 3,200 × 2.0 = 6,400 | 3,205 × 2.0 = 6,410 |
| SOL-USDC | 180 × 20 = 3,600 | 181 × 20 = 3,620 |
| AVAX-USDC | 40 × 50 = 2,000 | 40.5 × 50 = 2,025 |
| LINK-USDC | 20 × 100 = 2,000 | 20.2 × 100 = 2,020 |

Total quoted value = 6,000 + 6,010 + 6,400 + 6,410 + 3,600 + 3,620 + 2,000 + 2,025 + 2,000 + 2,020 = **40,085 USDC**

Credit utilization = 40,085 / 200,000 = **20%** — well within bounds with substantial room to add more quotes.
