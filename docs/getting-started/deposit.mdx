Before you can place orders on Vela, you need a balance in your account. During the public beta, Vela uses a trust-based deposit model — the exchange operator credits your account balance after you initiate a deposit.

<Warning>
**Beta deposit model:** Deposits in the current beta are **not settled on-chain**. Your balance is held by the exchange operator and tracked in Vela's state layer. On-chain deposit contracts are on the [M6 roadmap](/resources/roadmap). Do not deposit funds you cannot afford to lose.
</Warning>

## How to Deposit (Beta)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your wallet">
    Make sure your wallet is connected. See [Connect Your Wallet](/getting-started/connect-wallet) if you haven't done this yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Deposit page">
    Click your address in the top navigation and select **Deposit**, or navigate to the **Account** section from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select asset and amount">
    Choose the asset you want to deposit and enter the amount. The UI will show your connected wallet's balance for that asset.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the deposit">
    Click **Deposit**. In beta, this sends a signed deposit request to the Vela server. Your wallet signs the request to authenticate it — no on-chain transaction is submitted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Balance credited">
    Your Vela account balance updates immediately. You can see the new balance in the account sidebar and on the Balances page.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Viewing Your Balances

Your balances are visible in multiple places:

- **Account sidebar** — shown while trading, updates in real time as orders fill
- **Balances page** — full breakdown of all assets with available vs. reserved (in open orders) amounts
- **API** — `GET /account/:address/balances` returns your full balance snapshot

### Available vs. Reserved

| Balance Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Total** | All funds credited to your account |
| **Available** | Funds not locked in open orders — can be used for new orders |
| **Reserved** | Funds locked as order value in resting limit orders |

When you place a limit order to buy 1 ETH at 3200 USDC, 3200 USDC moves from Available to Reserved. When the order fills, the Reserved USDC is consumed and you receive 1 ETH. If you cancel the order, the 3200 USDC returns to Available.

## Withdrawals

Withdrawals in beta work the same way as deposits: you submit a signed withdrawal request to the server. The server debits your balance and records the withdrawal. On-chain settlement (actual transfer to your wallet) is on the M6 roadmap.

<Warning>
Withdrawals in beta do **not** transfer funds to your wallet immediately. The withdrawal is recorded in Vela's state layer. On-chain withdrawal settlement requires the smart contract work in M6.
</Warning>

## What Changes at Mainnet

At mainnet (M6), the deposit model changes to on-chain settlement:

1. A Solidity deposit contract is deployed
2. You call `deposit()` on the contract with your funds
3. The contract emits a `Deposited` event
4. The Vela engine observes the event and credits your account
5. Withdrawals submit a request that the engine approves, and the contract releases funds to your wallet

This makes deposits and withdrawals trustless — the operator cannot prevent you from withdrawing.
